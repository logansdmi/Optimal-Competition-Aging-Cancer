Optimal Control of Aging and Cancer

Logan de Monchaux-Irons 2021

 

This is the home page for a project to find optimal strategies for combating aging and cancer. A preformulated mathematical model will be used, it describes how cooperation and competition relate to the complex dynamics of aging and cancer in multicellular organisms

Introduction

Cooperation is essential for multicellular life. cells offer some of their own resources to the upkeep of the whole organism at the cost of less resources for the individual cell.

Over time healthy cells lose fitness, eventually loosing the ability to reproduce and become senescent. Intercellular competition is needed so that healthy cells can stop the accumulation of senescent cells which is related to aging related diseases.

However if cells mutate so that they no longer have the cost of cooperation they have a selective advantage over healthy cells. This can lead to uncontrolled replication of the non cooperative cells which is a main feature of cancer.

My project involves extending the mathematical model to include intervention strategies such as removal and reverting mutations. This lets us see how interventions can affect the whole system. An important part of real therapies is finding the optimal timing of the therapies. Optimal control theory can be used to find optimal protocols in a dynamical system both analytically and numerically. This will be applied to the model by making a cost function based on the cost of toxicity of the strategies then manipulating the state variables over time to find an optimality criterion of better vitality of the organism. This will then be compared with current real protocols.

Another method of finding optimal protocols in a dynamical system is through reinforcement learning. We will specifically use Q learning where an intelligence agent performs a set of actions on the system through time, each action has a cost and benefit and the agent performs the action which has the best benefit for future states. This method will then be compared to the optimal control method and the real life strategies

 

Mathematical Model

Fitness is a measure of how well a cell can reproduce and it depends on the vigor V (availability of resources), cooperation c (how much of the resources the cell gives others), base reproduction rate f0 and the fraction k which is the associated cost of cooperation. It forms an equation:

 

fv,c=f0v(1−kc)

For healthy cells (h): v=c=1

For cancerous cells (c): v=1,c=0

For senescent cells (s): v=0,c=1

For cells that are both senescent and cancerous (b): v=c=0

 

Mutations that cause senescence occur when cells go from v=1→0, this happens at a rate μv. Cancer mutations occur when cells go from c=1→0, this happens at a rate μc. This forms the master equation:

 

δNv,cδt=fv,cNv,c+μvNv+1,c−μvNv,c+μcNv,c+1−μcNv,c

 

The first term on the RHS accounts for the base growth rate of the genotype and the other terms account for mutations.

This project deals with the optimal procedures for reducing cancerous and senescent cells. Two methods for this are removal of cells and reversal of mutations.

A new parameter βv,c is introduced to set removal rates of the different types of cells.

 

dNv,cdt=(rv,c)Nv,c+mutations

where: ri=fi−βi

Reversion of mutations is accounted for by changing the sign of any mutation rate term I.e. +μv→−μv for reversal of senescence causing mutations.
